Roles

Job title and role don't always correspond to each other. Here's a list of the roles I've had in my career.

User Research

I've performed on site interviews with domestic and international customers. I've examined user behavior, created persona, and crafted user scenarios used to focus product road maps and prioritize new features.

Product Management

I've managed product road maps, release schedules, and sprints. I've sat between executives and developers, and worked through differences between what's desired (What can we sell) and what's possible (What can we build).

UX Design

I've established high level design patterns for products, and ensured those patterns were consistently implemented. I've set up both UX and Style guidelines, and I've championed reusable components that adhere to those guidelines.

UX Architect

I've built future looking demos used on the main stage at large conferences. I've crafted road map presentations for Gartner and large customers. I've establish long term product vision for new product, and I've revised existing product direction based on industry trends.

Visual Design

I've designed corporate identities, websites, business cards, and info graphics. I've created product themes, accessible color palettes, data visualizations, and icons... lots of icons. Regardless of my role, I've put my visual design skills to good use.

Client Developer

Mock ups will only go so far. If you want to truly influence user experience, then sometimes you have to roll up your sleeves and get your hands dirty in the source code. I like providing tangible support to dev teams as they crank out features on tight deadlines, usually in the form of CSS and/or DOM manipulation.
